Rumi at a glance

Maulana Rumi

Jalāl ad-Dīn Muhammad Rūmī (1207–1273) was a 13th-century Persian poet, Islamic scholar, and Sufi mystic. His encounter with Shams of Tabriz—a wandering dervish—served as the turning point in Rumi's life. Shams sparked a spiritual and emotional awakening in Rumi, driving him away from traditional scholarship and into an all-consuming quest for divine love.

Divan Shams Tabrizi

Rumi's Divan-e Shams-e Tabrizi stands as a powerful testament to his devotion to Shams of Tabriz and explores themes of divine love, longing, and ecstatic union. It features thousands of lyrical poems (ghazals) that brim with passion and metaphysical insight. Meanwhile, his Masnavi, is a monumental six-volume spiritual guide composed in verse, filled with parables and stories that illuminate the soul's path to the Divine.

Whirling Dervishes

The Whirling Dervishes perform the Sema, a symbolic and meditative dance that embodies the quest for spiritual enlightenment and union with the Divine. Inspired by Rumi's teachings, the Sema involves graceful spinning movements that represent the rotation of the universe and the soul's ascent towards God.
